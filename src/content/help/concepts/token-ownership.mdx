The guestbook and puzzles don't need an account. So how do they know which notes are yours?

Your browser remembers. When you add an entry, the site saves a small ownership marker (sometimes called a "token") inside your browser. Next time you visit from the same browser, it sees the marker and shows edit and delete buttons on your entries.

## What you need to know

<Checklist>
  <ChecklistItem>Use the **same browser** on the **same device** to keep editing.</ChecklistItem>
  <ChecklistItem>If you clear your browsing data, you'll lose the marker — the entries stay, but the edit buttons go away.</ChecklistItem>
  <ChecklistItem>Private/incognito windows don't remember between sessions.</ChecklistItem>
  <ChecklistItem>Switching phones or computers means you can't edit past entries from the new device.</ChecklistItem>
</Checklist>

<Callout type="info" title="Want it to follow you everywhere?">
  Features like the [blog](/blog) and [gallery](/gallery) use your [account](/help/getting-started/accounts-and-login) instead, so you can edit from anywhere you're signed in.
</Callout>
