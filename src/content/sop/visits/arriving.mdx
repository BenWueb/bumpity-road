## Before You Arrive (Winter)

<Callout type="info" title="Call ahead in winter">
  It's a good idea to call [**Rick**](/sop/reference/contacts#rick-craig) (our
  cabin check and plow guy) at **218-200-9420** to let him know we'll be up.
</Callout>

## Heating

<Steps>
  <Step title="Check the gas fireplace thermostat">
    Take note of the current temperature reading. **55°F and higher is OK.** If
    it's **lower than 50°F**, check whether the base electric heat is working or
    the fireplace needs new batteries.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Know the energy-saving plan">
    We're on an energy-saving plan with [**Crow Wing
    Power**](/sop/reference/contacts#crow-wing-power). Throughout the
    winter they turn the electric heat off during peak times (for example, Jan 6
    from 3:30 to 8:30). You can go online to see when these peak times are
    scheduled.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Turn the gas fireplace on">
    Turn the gas fireplace on to heat the cabin — set it up to about **70°F**. If
    it doesn't turn on at the wall thermostat, turn it on manually with the
    ON/OFF button. See the gas fireplace guide for details.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Turn up the electric baseboard heat">
    Turn electric heat up in the master bedroom and other rooms being used,
    including bathrooms. **60°F is a good starting point.** Check that the
    baseboards are working — they should feel a little warm.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="danger" title="Fire safety">
  Make sure nothing is close to the base heaters that could start a fire. Keep
  furniture and objects clear of the baseboards and the gas fireplace.
</Callout>

## Water

<Steps>
  <Step title="Turn the water on">
    Follow the water system guide to turn the water on at the filters and main
    line.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check the faucets">
    Make sure all faucets and showers are turned off — they could have been left
    open when the last person drained the pipes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check for leaks">
    Check for water leaks, especially in the kitchen and bathrooms.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Turn the water heater on">
    Turn the water heat on at the electric panel breakers.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Water softener">
    Plug the water softener in and check whether you need to add more salt.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Dishwasher first run">
  If you use the [dishwasher](/sop/appliances/dishwasher), run it during the
  **day** the first time, just in case it leaks, so you'll be around to catch it.
</Callout>
