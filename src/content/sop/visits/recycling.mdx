## Garbage

We use [**City Sanitary Service**](/sop/reference/contacts#city-sanitary-service).
Garbage goes in the **red bags** (about $6 each).

## Best Practices

- When going into town, remember to take the recycling with your trip so it
  doesn't pile up on the porch and get the floor/grout dirty.
- Rinse each container before placing it in the recycle bin to take to town.
- Divide the recycling into the three streams below before heading to the
  recycling center.

## Sort Into Three Streams

### 1. Blue Recycling Container

For **cans, plastic bottles (only #1 through #6), and glass bottles & jars**.

- Throw away lids and caps
- Rinse everything out

<Callout type="danger" title="Do NOT put these in the blue container">
  No plastic bags, plastic film or food wrap, Styrofoam, oil or pesticide
  containers, or broken plastic products without a recycling symbol.
</Callout>

### 2. White Recycling Bin

For **clean paper only** — mail, boxboard, newspaper, and magazines.

### 3. Plastic Bags

Plastic grocery bags go in the dedicated plastic-bag bins (there's a bin in
Hackensack, or at the grocery stores).

## Drop-Off Sites

1. Take it back home and recycle there.
2. **Hackensack** — by the Southside Gas Station.
3. **Longville** — parking lot behind the library.
