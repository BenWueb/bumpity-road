## How to Turn the Water On

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the filter knobs">
    Turn the water filter knobs on **both** filter containers from off to on.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the line to the filter system">
    Open the water line that feeds the filter system.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Slowly open the main water line">
    Open the main water line **slowly**. Be ready to turn it back off — along with
    the individual water filter knobs — if a filter container leaks.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="If a filter container leaks">
  The filter container may need to be tightened, or the O-ring may need to be
  replaced or adjusted. The filter containers **shouldn't be too tight** — don't
  overtighten them.
</Callout>

## After the Water Is On — Check Quickly

1. Check all faucets to make sure they are off.
2. Check for water leaks — **kitchen and bathrooms**.

<Callout type="warning" title="Dishwasher first run">
  If using the [dishwasher](/sop/appliances/dishwasher), run it during the **day**
  the first time, just in case it leaks.
</Callout>

## Pressure / Well Tank

The water pressure is maintained by the well tank. The bladder pump was replaced
in May 2024.

**How it works:** the pump fills the tank and compresses the air inside until the
pressure switch shuts the pump off. As water is used, pressure drops and the pump
kicks back on. This keeps water pressure steady and reduces how often the pump
runs. The tank's air **pre-charge** is normally set about **2 psi below the pump
cut-in pressure** (e.g., 38 psi for a 40/60 switch) — only a qualified
professional should check or adjust it. Don't let the tank freeze.

## Water Softener

Plug the water softener in and check whether you need to add more salt.

## Water Heater Power

The water heater is powered from the **electric panel breakers** — turn the water
heat on there after the water is running.

## Turning the Water Off

When closing up, the water gets turned off downstairs and the pipes drained. See
the Winterizing guide for the full shut-down and antifreeze procedure.
