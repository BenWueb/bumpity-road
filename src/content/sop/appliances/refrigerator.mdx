## Basic Operation

<Steps>
  <Step title="Temperature controls">
    The fresh-food and freezer controls are numbered dials — **0 is off, 9 is
    coldest** (setting 0 does not cut power to the unit). A mid setting on each is
    a good starting point.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Adjusting">
    After changing a setting, wait **24 hours** for the temperature to stabilize.
    If you need to adjust, move the control **one number at a time** and wait 12
    hours between changes.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Crispers & Water

- **Crisper humidity:** slide to **High** for most vegetables, **Low** for most
  fruits.
- **Water filter:** replace the filter cartridge about **every 6 months** for
  clean water and ice.

<Callout type="warning" title="Care">
  Do not clean cold glass shelves with warm water — a sudden temperature change
  can shatter them. Unplug the fridge before cleaning or changing a light bulb.
</Callout>

## Troubleshooting

- **Fridge or freezer too warm** — make sure a package isn't holding a door open;
  set the control one step colder; allow 24 hours after any change to cool down.
- **Runs constantly** — normal when first plugged in or after adding lots of food;
  modern fridges cycle often to hold steady temps.
- **Icemaker not working** — make sure the icemaker power switch is **ON** and the
  water supply is connected; give the freezer 24 hours to get cold; level piled-up
  cubes by hand.
- **Water or ice dispenser not working** — check the water supply, replace a
  clogged filter, and note the dispenser may be **LOCKED** (press and hold the
  Lock pad for 3 seconds). Air in a new system clears by holding the dispenser arm
  for ~2 minutes.
- **Small or hollow ice cubes / slow water** — replace the clogged water filter.
- **Odor** — wrap foods well, keep an open box of baking soda inside, and clean
  the interior.
- **Water on the floor** — the defrost drain may be clogged (see the manual's
  care & cleaning steps).
- **Buzzing sound** — if the icemaker switch is ON but the water line isn't
  connected, switch the icemaker **OFF** to avoid damaging the water valve.

<Callout type="info" title="Normal sounds">
  Humming, whooshing when doors close, clicking/popping, and gurgling water
  sounds are all normal for this fridge.
</Callout>

## Leaving the Cabin

When closing up, clean the fridge and toss out-of-date items, but leave it **on**
at a normal setting. See the [Closing the Cabin](/sop/visits/closing) checklist.
