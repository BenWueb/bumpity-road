## Lighting

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the lid first">
    Always open the lid before igniting. Make sure all burner knobs are **off**
    and the gas supply is on.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ignite left to right">
    Push and hold a burner knob in for ~2 seconds, then turn to **start/high**
    until it snaps and lights. Light the burners left to right. If a burner
    doesn't light within ~4 seconds, turn it off and wait 5 minutes before trying
    again.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Preheat">
    With the lid closed, preheat all burners on high for **10–15 minutes**. Cook
    with the lid closed to build heat and avoid flare-ups.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Turning Off

Turn all burner knobs to **off**, then **close the propane / gas supply valve**.

## Care & Safety

- Check the slide-out **grease tray before each use** and clear built-up grease
  (grease-fire risk).
- If a grease fire starts, **turn off the burners and keep the lid closed** until
  it's out.
- Check for gas leaks with **soapy water — never a flame**.
- Replace the gas hose about **every 5 years**.

## Troubleshooting

- **Burner won't ignite** — first check there's gas flow by lighting it with a
  **match** (matchstick holder is under the left side table). If it lights with a
  match, the **igniter** is the problem (call service). If it won't light even with
  a match, it's a **gas-flow** problem — reconnect the propane cylinder and make
  sure the valve is open.
- **No ignition after trying** — turn the knob off and wait **5 minutes** for gas
  to clear before trying again.
- **Yellow, lazy flames or a gas smell** — burner ports or the spider/insect
  screens are likely clogged; clean them.
- **Low or uneven heat, food sticking** — time for a cleaning: clear grease, the
  Flavorizer bars, and the burner ports.
- **Low flame / not getting hot** — check the propane tank isn't empty.

<Callout type="warning" title="Propane & outdoor use">
  Only use the grill outdoors, away from the cabin. Check the propane connection
  for leaks before lighting, and close the propane valve when you're done.
</Callout>
