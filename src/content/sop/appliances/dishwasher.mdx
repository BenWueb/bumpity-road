## Basic Operation

1. Scrape food debris off dishes before loading
2. Load dishes with dirty side facing the center spray arm
3. Add detergent pod to the dispenser on the door
4. Select **Normal Wash** for everyday loads
5. Press **Start** and close the door firmly

## Loading Tips

- **Top rack**: cups, glasses, small bowls, and plastics
- **Bottom rack**: plates, pots, pans, and cutting boards
- **Silverware basket**: alternate forks, knives, and spoons for best cleaning

<Callout type="warning" title="No Wooden Items">
  Never put wooden cutting boards, utensils, or items with wooden handles in the dishwasher. Hand wash only.
</Callout>

## Cleaning the Dishwasher

Run a cleaning cycle once per visit:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Empty the dishwasher">
    Make sure the dishwasher is completely empty.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Clean the filter">
    Twist and remove the filter at the bottom. Rinse under hot water and scrub with an old toothbrush.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wipe the door seal">
    Use a damp cloth to wipe the rubber gasket around the door. Check for trapped food.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run a vinegar cycle">
    Place a cup of white vinegar on the top rack. Run a hot cycle with nothing else inside.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Likely Cause | Fix |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| Dishes not clean | Overloaded or filter clogged | Reduce load, clean filter |
| Water pooling at bottom | Drain clogged | Clear drain area, check filter |
| Detergent not dispensing | Dispenser jammed | Clean dispenser latch |
| Strange odor | Food buildup | Run vinegar cleaning cycle |
