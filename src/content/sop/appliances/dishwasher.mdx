## Starting a Wash

<Steps>
  <Step title="Power on">Press the **ON/OFF** button.</Step>
  <Step title="Pick a cycle">Choose a cycle button (see below).</Step>
  <Step title="Start">Press **Start**. The **Clean** light comes on when the cycle finishes.</Step>
</Steps>

## Cycles & Options

- **Normal** — the most economical cycle; best for everyday full loads.
- **Auto** — sensor-controlled for mixed loads of average dirtiness.
- **Heavy** — pots, pans, and stubborn baked-on food.
- **Speed60** — a faster ~1-hour wash.
- **Sanitize** option adds extra-hot rinsing for higher hygiene.

<Callout type="info" title="AutoAir drying">
  The door **pops open by itself** during the drying phase — that's normal. For
  best drying, wait until the cycle fully finishes (no lights lit) before
  unloading.
</Callout>

## Loading & Supplies

- **Top rack:** cups, glasses, and small items (height-adjustable via RackMatic).
- **Bottom rack:** pans and plates.
- **Silverware:** load with sharp points **down**.
- Add detergent to the dispenser and keep the **rinse aid** filled for better
  drying.

## Troubleshooting

- **Dishes not clean / food left on** — don't overfill or nest items, make sure
  the spray arms can spin freely, and clean the filter and spray-arm nozzles. Pick
  a more intensive cycle, and **don't pre-rinse heavily** (the sensor then picks a
  lighter cycle).
- **Dishes not dry** — refill the **rinse aid**, use a cycle with a drying phase,
  and wait until the cycle fully ends (about 30 min) before unloading.
- **White film or spots** — usually hard-water/detergent residue; use rinse aid
  and a more intensive program.
- **Excess suds or leaking** — only ever use **dishwasher detergent** (never hand
  or dish soap). Wipe up any spilled rinse aid.
- **All four vertical lights are ON (drain issue)** — check the drain hose isn't
  kinked, the garbage-disposal knockout plug is removed, and the water faucet is
  on / inlet filter is clear.
- **All four vertical lights are FLASHING (error)** — turn it off, cut power at the
  plug or breaker, wait 2 minutes, then power back on. If it repeats, call for
  service.

## First Run of the Visit

<Callout type="warning" title="Run it during the day the first time">

  it leaks, so you'll be around to catch it. See [Arriving at the
  Cabin](/sop/visits/arriving) and the [Water System](/sop/systems/water-system).
</Callout>
