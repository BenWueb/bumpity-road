## Overview

Before leaving the cabin for cold weather, the water must be drained and the
traps protected so the pipes don't freeze and burst.

<Callout type="danger" title="Critical">
  Skipping these steps can lead to frozen, burst pipes and serious water damage.
  Follow them carefully.
</Callout>

## Drain the Water

<Steps>
  <Step title="Turn the water off downstairs">
    Turn the water off downstairs and drain the pipes. See the instructional sheet
    at the cabin and the Water System guide.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Drain the basement faucet">
    Drain the water out of the faucet next to the basement shower and toilet. Then
    **remember to close it again**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Drain the washing machine">
    Turn off the water to the washing machine (hot and cold) and drain the hoses.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## RV Antifreeze

<Steps>
  <Step title="Toilet">
    Pour RV pink antifreeze into the toilet **bowl and tank**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Pink RV antifreeze only">
  Use the non-toxic pink RV antifreeze. It protects the traps and the toilet from
  freezing.
</Callout>

## Heat

Leave the heat set at **45-50°F** so the cabin stays above freezing without
wasting energy.

  See the Closing the Cabin checklist for the rest of the shut-down steps
  (locking windows, base heater clearance, doors, lights, etc.).
</Callout>
